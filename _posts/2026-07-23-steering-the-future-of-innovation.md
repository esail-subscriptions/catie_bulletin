Higher education is evolving faster than ever, and at the heart of that transformation across Texas is **Rolando R. Garza, Ed.D.** Stepping into his new role as Chair of the **Council on Academic Technology & Innovative Education (CATIE)**, Dr. Garza brings a dynamic blend of visionary leadership, deep technical expertise, and a steadfast commitment to student equity.

When asked about taking on the leadership of CATIE, Dr. Garza shared his vision for the road ahead:

> "As the incoming Chair of the Council on Academic Technology & Innovative Education (CATIE), I am truly honored to serve alongside the pioneers, go-getters, and innovators who are transforming teaching, learning, and student success across the Texas A&M System. We are at a defining moment in higher education, where our collective leadership and collaboration can shape the future of academic innovation. My vision is to strengthen the connections between our member institutions, champion the thoughtful integration of emerging technologies, and create opportunities to learn from one another. Together, we will embrace innovation with purpose, empower our faculty and students, and build a stronger, future-ready Texas A&M System through collaboration, service, and excellence."

## Rooted in Javelina Nation Excellence

Dr. Garza’s leadership in academic technology is well-established. As the **Director of the Center for Academic Technology** within the division of Academic Affairs at **Texas A&M University-Kingsville (TAMUK)**, he leads a team he proudly calls his **“Dream Team.”** Together, they continually push the envelope of educational innovation throughout **Javelina Nation**.

Holding an **Ed.D. in Education from Nova Southeastern University**—with a specialized concentration in **distance education and instructional technology**—Dr. Garza connects big-picture policy with practical classroom application.

### Highlights of Dr. Garza's Career & Impact

- **The HEART Project ($5M Title III HSI STEM Grant):** As Co-Project Investigator, Dr. Garza helps lead a 5-year initiative aimed at providing wrap-around student services and high-impact pedagogical training for faculty. The program directly supports Hispanic, low-income, and transfer students across 12 STEM majors where pre-COVID retention rates had dipped below **79.1%**.

- **Texas A&M System Leadership:** Prior to assuming the role of Chair, Dr. Garza served on the CATIE Executive Committee as **Legislative Committee Chair**.

- **Statewide Governance:** He actively serves on the **Learning Technology Advisory Committee (LTAC)** for the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board (THECB).

- **National & Regional Distance Learning Advocacy:** A past president (2015–2018) and longtime leader in the **Texas Distance Learning Association (TxDLA)**, Dr. Garza also serves on the Executive Board of the **United States Distance Learning Association (USDLA)** as Chair of State Chapters.

With a proven track record of bridging technology, faculty development, and student success, Dr. Garza’s tenure as CATIE Chair promises to unlock exciting new chapters for academic innovation across the entire Texas A&M System.
